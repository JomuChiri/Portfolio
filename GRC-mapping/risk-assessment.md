1. Title
  Risk Assessment – [System/Scenario] – [Date]

2. Objective
  Goal: Identify, evaluate, and prioritize risks affecting the SOC lab or SME environment.
  Why: Risk assessments demonstrate governance maturity and align with compliance frameworks (ISO 27005, NIST RMF).
  Scenario: Example – Assess risks in a Windows endpoint monitored by Wazuh + Sysmon.

3. Environment Overview
  Scope: Systems, applications, and processes under review.
  Tools: Wazuh SIEM, Sysmon telemetry, vulnerability scanners (e.g., OpenVAS, Nessus).
  Frameworks: NIST RMF, ISO 27005, CIS Controls.
  Data Sources: Logs, vulnerability reports, incident history.

4. Risk Identification
  List potential threats (phishing, brute force, malware, insider misuse).
  Identify vulnerabilities (unpatched systems, weak passwords, misconfigured firewall).
  Document assets at risk (endpoints, servers, user accounts, sensitive data).

5. Risk Analysis
  Assess likelihood (Low/Medium/High).
  Assess impact (Low/Medium/High).
  Use a risk matrix to visualize.
  Example:
    Threat: Phishing → Likelihood: High, Impact: Medium.
    Threat: Credential dumping → Likelihood: Medium, Impact: High.

6. Risk Evaluation
  Prioritize risks based on combined score (likelihood × impact).
  Highlight critical risks requiring immediate mitigation.
  Document rationale for prioritization.

7. Risk Treatment
  Mitigation: Apply patches, enforce MFA, update detection rules.
  Avoidance: Disable unused services.
  Transfer: Consider cyber insurance.
  Acceptance: Document low‑impact risks.

8. Monitoring & Review
  Schedule recurring risk assessments.
  Automate vulnerability scans.
  Feed new incidents back into risk register.
  Update playbooks and compliance documentation.

9. Evidence & Screenshots
  Risk matrix diagram.
  Vulnerability scan report snippet.
  Wazuh alert screenshot.
  Risk register table.

10. Outcome
  Risks identified, analyzed, and prioritized.
  Mitigation strategies documented.
  Compliance alignment demonstrated.
  Recruiter‑friendly artifact showing governance maturity.
 
11. Recruiter‑Friendly Summary
  “This case study demonstrates my ability to conduct structured risk assessments aligned with NIST RMF and ISO 27005. I identified threats, analyzed likelihood and impact, prioritized risks, and documented mitigation strategies—showcasing both technical depth and governance awareness.”
